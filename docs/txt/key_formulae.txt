## Summary of correspondences

- **Metric ↔ Medium structure:**  
  - **GR:** Metric tensor \(g_{\mu\nu}\) encodes curvature and causal structure.  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Index mapping \(n(\rho_s)\) and retarded vector flow \(\mathbf{u}_g\) encode optical geometry and gravito‑magnetism.

- **Geodesics ↔ Fermat paths:**  
  - **GR:** Test bodies/light follow geodesics derived from \(\Gamma^\mu_{\alpha\beta}\).  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Light follows stationary‑time paths from Fermat’s principle in a moving medium; massive motion follows the substrate push law consistent with PPN.

- **Connections ↔ Gradients:**  
  - **GR:** Christoffel symbols from metric gradients.  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Deflection from \(\nabla n\) and advection via \(\nabla \mathbf{u}_g\).

- **Einstein equations ↔ Substrate PDEs:**  
  - **GR:** \(G_{\mu\nu}=8\pi T_{\mu\nu}\).  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Symmetric‑hyperbolic PDEs for \((n,\mathbf{v},h^{\mathrm{TT}})\) with energy norm and conservation.

- **TT waves ↔ TT wave channel:**  
  - **GR:** Tensor, transverse–traceless GWs, \(v_g=c\).  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Linear TT wave sector with \(\Box h^{\mathrm{TT}}=\mathcal{S}^{\mathrm{TT}}\), \(v_g=c\).

## Interpretation differences

- **Geometry vs medium:**  
  - **GR:** Gravity is curvature; light cones from metric.  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Gravity is effective optics in a physical medium; causality from characteristic speeds with \(c_s\le c\) and TT waves at \(c\).

- **Degrees of freedom:**  
  - **GR:** Metric components subject to constraints/gauge.  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Scalar density \(n\), momentum \(n\mathbf{v}\), and TT field \(h^{\mathrm{TT}}\) with a block‑diagonal symmetrizer.

- **Gravito‑magnetism:**  
  - **GR:** Encoded in off‑diagonal metric components and vector potential.  
  - **Pushing‑medium:** Explicit retarded flow \(\mathbf{u}_g\) (translation) and \(\omega_s\) (rotation) with Fizeau‑type coupling to photons.
